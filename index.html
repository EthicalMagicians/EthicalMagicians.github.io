Heirlooms of Aeons is a project that sets out to encourage participants to reflect on the impact of their actions. We wanted to focus on these impacts over time, especially in reference to generations. To this end, we worked toward creating a world built upon narrative, a collection of art, and a game demo centred on themes of time.

The concept was influenced by the fictional illness ‘Hanahaki’ disease in its portrayal of the natural world taking over the human form over time. This area of experimentation attempted to reconcile the relationship between our concept of time and guardianship or ‘Kaitiakitanga’. People would ‘become a tree’ as time passed, the tree remaining as an indicator of their existence. The concept art explores possible ways that could happen - how a vine or tree could become intertwined with the human form. The world changing events of 2020 caused us to pivot - we decided to eschew this concept in reflecting on the covid-situation, elements that could be considered a representation of a plague seemed insensitive. The world design shifted to become a depopulated hamlet featuring trees in unlikely places, some as if apparently grown as if magically overnight. Later pieces investigate the use of colour and feature a more fully conceptualised take on the tree as an overarching ‘world tree’ element.
